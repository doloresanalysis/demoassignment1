Project Idea

Overview

Inspired by the efforts of wildlife conservationists, park rangers, and animal rights activists. Sentinel is an AI drone surveillance network that will help combat poaching, further study animals, and promote animal rights. The drones will be programmed with AI to help identify poaching behaviour in national parks and track criminal activity. The project’s success will help protect countless animals from poachers and the black market, as well as further our study of animal behaviour. The main challenges for this project is the cost efficiency, drone battery life, and effective algorithm in identifying poaching behaviour.

Motivation

Drones have a variety of uses in the real world, from being used to photograph scenic views to even being trialled to patrol national borders. From an educational perspective, we have yet to fully understand animal behaviour and their migration patterns, and the use of drones will help us learn more. A statistic by the World Wide Fund for Nature (WWF) reported rhino poaching in South Africa increased from 13 to 1004 between 2007 to 2013 - an increase of 7700%. As a result, some species of rhinos are critically endangered and risk being extinct. This is only an example of one animal out of numerous in danger of being wiped out. Although the efforts made today to prevent and tackle poaching are admirable, it is not enough. Drones can be the answer to help put an end to poaching.

Description

  • Features
  • Speed up to 100mph and flight action around 1 hour
  • Equipped with powerful infrared camera
  • Poaching identifying AI algorithm
  • 'Lock and Track' function
  • Silent Alarm System
  • Zenmuse X5 camera

Sentinel is an AI drone surveillance network that will help combat poaching, further the study of animals, and promote animal rights. The project was inspired by the efforts of wildlife conservationists and park rangers around the globe, and will provide an invaluable tool to them if the project comes to fruition. The drones will be programmed with an AI algorithm that will identify poaching behaviour in national parks and track criminal activity. The project’s main challenges arise from cost efficiency, drone production, and developing an algorithm that will be effective and reliable.

Drones have a variety of uses out in the real world; from being used to photograph scenic views to patrol national borders. A statistic by the World Wide Fund for Nature (WWF) reported rhino poaching in South Africa increased from 13 to 1004 between 2007 to 2013 - an increase of 7700%. Consequently some rhino species are critically endangered, and this is only one example of the many species of animals that are in danger of being extinct. Although admirable efforts are being made by concerned stakeholders, it is simply not enough. Drones can be the answer to finally put an end to poaching.

Speed & Flight Action

Currently, we rely on park rangers to patrol national parks to deter poachers but they need to cover huge areas: the Kruger National Park in South Africa is 19,485 square kilometres. Therefore, it’s not feasible for them to patrol by foot/vehicle and drones can provide an effective alternative since they can cover a lot of ground in a short amount of time. High-end consumer-grade drones can fly up to about 80 km/h and can only be in the air for about 25 minutes. To improve these technical limitations, Sentinel will use combustion engines which will allow them to fly faster, longer, and even carry more weight. These drones will have better manoeuvrability than ground patrols as they’re in the air and can bypass obstructions from terrains and roaming animals.

Infrared/Thermal Camera

Additionally, drones will be equipped with powerful infrared/thermal cameras that can detect heat signatures from people, animals, and gunfire. Since drones can detect heat signatures from animals, it can help us learn about animal behaviour by understanding their migratory patterns more closely and accurately. Experts today still don’t fully understand elephants and their reasoning for migrating to certain locations and drone surveillance can provide valuable data.

AI Algorithm

Along with an AI algorithm with multiple functions, such as poaching identification, ‘lock and track’ function, and silent alarm system, we can effectively deal with poachers and further undermine black markets. Drones will be equipped with powerful cameras that can allow them to visually recognise poaching behaviour, i.e. shooting rifle postures, handling an animal’s carcass, etc. The algorithm will then trigger a silent alarm system alerting nearby park rangers and law enforcement agencies. Afterwards, the drones will be “locked” onto the target and then continually follow the poachers so they can be either arrested or tracked until more criminal accomplices can be caught.

Commercial Purposes

The drones’ highly sophisticated functions can also be used for personal and commercial purposes. The national park can offer a paid service to photograph, film and study animals and the surrounding scenery. This can be quite useful to photographers, cinematographers and wildlife experts who may not have access to such high end equipment. As a result, the drones program will be generating an additional source of income for the national park which can further fund the project, national park facilities and wildlife conservation efforts, justifying their use.

Overall, the drones have a variety of uses that can benefit a range of stakeholders such as animal experts, wildlife conservationists, and the wider community so they can enjoy the prolonged conservation of some species.

Tools and Technologies

The drone model will be based on a quadcopter and use a combustion engine. A combustion engine will be used rather than conventional batteries because the former can improve the speed up to 100 km/h in comparison to battery-powered drones reaching around 80 km/h. Combustion engines also allow drones to stay significantly longer in the air than batteries: 60 minutes versus 25 minutes. Additionally, they can carry heavier loads of up to 5 kg which will be useful for equipping the drone with a thermal and high-end camera and other hardware.

The drone will be programmed with an AI algorithm that can recognise poaching behaviour, with certain postures such as aiming a rifle, sawing motion, etc. to trigger different levels of suspicion. Upon reaching higher levels of suspicion, the drone will proceed to closely inspect the situation so it can verify the activity. Using a GPS, the drone can then alert rangers and the police of the location (Silent Alarm System) and then “lock and track”.

Skills Required

Scientists from the University of Southern California have already developed an algorithm, called Systematic POacher deTector (SPOT), that can differentiate poachers from animals in three-tenths of a second. The next step would be to design an algorithm that can accurately and quickly recognise poaching behaviour which can be very complicated. For instance, the AI will need to be able to tell the difference between an individual holding a rifle and a camera, analysing the posture and body positioning. Time of day can influence the probability of poaching as poachers normally operate at night to avoid watchful eyes from tourists and rangers. We have the necessary hardware available and in reach of the software needed, therefore Sentinel is a feasible and viable project.

Outcome

The project’s success will effectively deter, prevent and tackle the increasingly problematic issue of poaching. We will be preserving our precious and fragile ecosystems by protecting endangered species, and will be supporting wildlife education by providing more data for experts and students to interpret and analyse. It will also give an opportunity for photographers and cinematographers to take pictures and film beautiful sceneries that may have not been possible before. By providing a stream of income by offering these services, the program can be self-funded to a certain extent and support the national park and wildlife conservation efforts. In conclusion, the project coming to light has a multitude of benefits to the many stakeholders that can justify the high costs of such an ambitious project.
